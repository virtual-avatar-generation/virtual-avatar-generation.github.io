Introduction

We present SABR, a video model capable of simulating human movement within a given environment by assuming the parameters of a virtual avatar. SABR, a diffusion transformer, is applied to the human motion domain, featuring two key design choices: predicting the sample instead of noise in each diffusion step, and ingesting entire videos to predict complete motion sequences. We focus on rock climbing due to its single-agent interaction and static environment, both of which make it an effective testbed for world navigation. This setting provides a controlled environment for clear evaluation and encompasses complex biomechanical interactions, which are also present, though to a lesser extent, in real-world scenarios. We aptly name our model SABR-CLIMB.

Safety

While our model shows promising results, it can sometimes suggest incorrect or unsafe moves, which may pose physical risks. Ensuring the model's accuracy and safety is a top priority.
